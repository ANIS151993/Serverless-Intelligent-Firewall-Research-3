Executive summary

ASLF-OSINT extends the original Serverless Intelligent Firewall research into an autonomous, self-learning, multi-cloud security architecture. The framework combines threat-intel APIs, hybrid risk scoring, and federated zero-trust policy control.

The core research gap is the lack of serverless-native systems that unify adaptive analytics, federated policy enforcement, and practical runtime orchestration across cloud and private infrastructure.

Research scope and methodology

The manuscript combines benchmark IDS datasets (CICIDS2017, CIC-IDS2018, UNSW-NB15, NSL-KDD) with 30 days of live threat-intel feeds from MISP, AlienVault OTX, and VirusTotal. The pipeline is designed for autonomous retraining under concept drift.

Threat-intel and data pipeline

  • REST API ingestion from MISP, AlienVault OTX, and VirusTotal.
  • Indicator normalization for IP/domain/hash intelligence.
  • Feature alignment with flow, identity, and policy context.
  • Continuous drift-sensitive buffering for autonomous retraining.
  • Cross-tenant telemetry aggregation through super control.

Autonomous learning engine

  • Reinforcement learning (PPO) for response policy optimization.
  • Continual learning (SSF) for concept drift adaptation.
  • Meta-learning (prototypical framework) for few-shot zero-day recognition.
  • Federated learning (FedNova) for privacy-preserving distributed updates.
  • Explainability layer via SHAP and LIME for analyst trust.
Design intent: each tenant runs a compact local subsystem for resilient protection, while the super-control plane orchestrates global policy and auto-upgrade propagation.

System architecture

Architecture figure

The manuscript’s main architecture figure shows the detection engine, orchestration layer, and unified control plane spanning AWS, Azure, and GCP.

Operational layers

  • Super control layer: provisions tenants, publishes upgrades, and aggregates fleet telemetry.
  • Tenant subsystem layer: keeps local protection active with reduced dependency on central control.
  • Federated policy layer: enforces synchronized zero-trust decisions across distributed nodes.
  • Provider execution layer: maps decisions into AWS/Azure/GCP/Proxmox actions.
